# Mortality and End-of-Life Prompts

Draft prompts based on research into hospice, palliative care, cancer, bereaved parents, sudden loss, and grief support.

These are main prompt drafts only. After review, accepted prompts should get follow-ups, mode/tone/depth placement, production IDs, and localization.

## Unsaid Love

- What is a love you assume people know, but may not have said plainly enough?
- Who would you want to hear "I love you" from while there is still time?
- What affection do you show through actions more than words?
- Who in your life might need to hear that they mattered to you?
- What kind of love feels hardest for you to say out loud?
- What would you want your family to know about how deeply you loved them?
- What would you want a close friend to know if you could only say one honest thing?
- Who has loved you in a way you did not fully understand at the time?
- What part of your love do you hope people remember after you are gone?
- What would change if you stopped waiting for the perfect moment to say it?

## Gratitude Before It Is Too Late

- Who has made your life easier in ways they may never know?
- What ordinary kindness still means more to you than people realize?
- Who would you thank for helping you become yourself?
- What is a debt of gratitude you have carried quietly?
- Who showed up for you when they did not have to?
- What would you want to thank your parents, children, siblings, or chosen family for?
- What small act of care would you want someone to know you never forgot?
- Who taught you something about love by the way they lived?
- What gratitude would feel unfinished if you never expressed it?
- What thank you would you want to leave behind in writing?

## Repair and Forgiveness

- What relationship would you want more peace with before the end of your life?
- What apology have you delayed because it feels too complicated?
- Who do you wish you had understood with more compassion?
- What old conflict matters less now than it once did?
- Is forgiveness still possible if a personal reunion is not?
- What do you still need to forgive yourself for?
- Who might be carrying pain from you that you have not fully acknowledged?
- What repair would be meaningful even if it did not fix everything?
- What grudge would you not want to take to the end of your life?
- What truth, if it were told, could help heal an old wound?

## Being Remembered Honestly

- What would you want people to remember about you that is not impressive, just true?
- What part of you would you want remembered with humor?
- What flaw would you hope people understood with kindness?
- What do you hope people would say you cared about most?
- What story about you would feel honest without making you seem perfect?
- What do you hope people remember about how you made them feel?
- What would you not want your life reduced to?
- What ordinary version of you would you want people to keep?
- What would it mean to be remembered accurately rather than ideally?
- What part of your life would you want someone to tell the truth about?

## Legacy

- What value would you most want to leave with the people who love you?
- What story from your life would you like to be retold to future generations?
- What tradition would you want someone to keep, change, or understand?
- What advice would you want your future family to have in your own words?
- What object, recipe, phrase, or habit carries part of your story?
- What would you want a child in your family to know about where they come from?
- What lesson did life teach you the hard way that might help someone else?
- What would you record if your voice could be saved for someone later?
- What would you want your friends to carry forward from your life?
- What kind of legacy feels loving rather than performative?

## The Ordinary Moments

- What ordinary moment with someone you love would you want to relive once more?
- What simple routine would people miss about you?
- What place holds more of your life than it appears to?
- What vacation or trip carries a memory you treasure?
- What small family tradition would you want remembered?
- What tiny friendship ritual means more to you than it looks like from outside?
- What is a normal day you did not know you would miss until later?
- What everyday sound, smell, or phrase brings someone back to you?
- What ordinary moment would you choose over a grand goodbye?
- What part of daily life feels sacred when you imagine it ending?
- What will you miss?
- What will you not miss?

## Fear of Being a Burden

- What kind of help is hardest for you to accept?
- What would you fear asking from the people who love you?
- When does needing care start to feel like losing yourself?
- What would make being cared for feel more dignified?
- Who would you trust to see you vulnerable without making you feel small?
- What burden do you imagine you are, even when others might not see it that way?
- What would you want caregivers to know about your pride?
- Does needing care bring you comfort or concern that you are asking too much?
- What small worry would you want to spare your family from?
- What would you need to say if you felt your care was costing too much?

## Fear of Dying Alone

- Who would you hope was near you at the end?
- What kind of presence would comfort you most when words were no longer enough?
- What does "not alone" mean to you?
- Who makes silence feel less frightening?
- What would you want the room around you to feel like?
- What would make solitude peaceful rather than lonely?
- Who would you want to hold your hand, literally or otherwise?
- What would you want someone to say if you were afraid?
- What fear comes up when you imagine your last moments?
- What kind of goodbye would help you feel accompanied?

## Fear of Suffering

- What kind of pain or loss of control frightens you most?
- What does comfort mean to you when life becomes physically hard?
- What would make a difficult ending feel more humane?
- What does dignity mean when someone is weak, sick, or dependent?
- What would you want your loved ones to know about your limits?
- How would you want people to balance hope with relief from suffering?
- What medical choice would you want discussed before a crisis?
- What would help you feel more normal during illness?
- What fear about suffering do you rarely say out loud?
- What would a peaceful death mean to you?

## The People Left Behind

- Who would you worry about most if you were gone?
- What would you want the people who love you to be free from carrying?
- What would you want your partner, family, or friends to know about living after loss?
- Who would need reassurance that they did enough?
- What would you want to say to someone who might blame themselves?
- What hope would you want to leave for the people who grieve you?
- Who would you trust to help hold your family together?
- What would you want your loved ones to keep doing without guilt?
- What would you want people to remember about you when grief is breaking their hearts?
- What kinds of permissions would you want to give the people left behind?

## Children and Missed Futures

- What future moment with a child in your life would you most want to witness?
- What advice would you want a child to hear when they are older?
- What would you want your children, nieces, nephews, or younger relatives to know about you?
- What milestone would hurt most to miss?
- What story would you want a child to be told about your love for them?
- What would you want to say to a child that they will understand when they are older?
- What part of their growing up would you wish you could guide from a distance?
- What blessing would you want to leave for a young person you love?
- What would you hope they never doubt about your love?
- What future joy would you want them to feel free to have?

## Death of a Child

- How can a family keep saying a child's name with love?
- What memory of a child deserves to be protected from being overshadowed by their death?
- What would it mean to honor a child's whole life, not only the loss?
- What object, story, or ritual might help a family feel connected to a child who died?
- How can friends support parents without trying to fix the grief?
- What do siblings need people to remember after a child dies?
- What would help a parent feel that their child's life still matters?
- How can love continue when the future that held it has changed?
- What should people understand about grief that does not end?
- What kindness might matter on birthdays, anniversaries, and ordinary days after a child dies?
- What can people say when they want to show you empathy?

## Sudden Loss and No Goodbye

- What feels unfinished when there was no chance to say goodbye?
- What question would you keep asking after a sudden loss?
- What would you wish the person knew in their final ordinary day?
- How does shock change the way grief arrives?
- What guilt might someone carry even when the death was not their fault?
- What goodbye would you create if time had not given you one?
- What does the mind keep replaying after an accident or sudden death?
- What support helps when someone is still trying to believe the loss is real?
- What would you want friends to understand about the weeks after everyone else moves on?
- What ritual could help hold what never got to be said?

## Anticipatory Grief

- What is already being grieved before someone is gone?
- What is hard about loving someone while watching them change?
- What does hope look like when cure is no longer the only hope?
- What conversations become possible when time feels limited?
- What conversations still feel impossible, even with warning?
- What does it mean to say goodbye slowly?
- How can a family make memories while also preparing for loss?
- What part of the person do you fight to keep seeing beyond the illness?
- What is painful about grieving while still being needed?
- What small mercy can exist inside a long goodbye?

## Friends Who Stay or Disappear

- Who do you think would show up if life became very hard?
- What makes people disappear when someone is dying or grieving?
- What kind of friend can sit with pain without trying to escape it?
- What would you want friends to keep inviting you into, even if you often said no?
- Who has stayed close during a season that made other people uncomfortable?
- What support from a friend would feel practical and loving?
- What would you want to tell a friend who is afraid of saying the wrong thing?
- What does it mean to keep being someone's friend when their life has changed?
- What kind of check-in would feel real rather than performative?
- Who would you want beside your family if you could not be there?

## Caregiving and Love

- What kind of care feels like love to you?
- What is beautiful and exhausting about caring for someone who is declining?
- What resentment might a caregiver feel ashamed to admit?
- What does devotion look like when it is repetitive and unseen?
- How can someone receive care without feeling only like a patient?
- What would you want to say to someone who cared for you at great cost?
- What part of caregiving changes a relationship forever?
- What does a caregiver need permission to feel?
- What would help a family share care more honestly?
- What tenderness can exist in the hardest practical tasks?

## Autonomy and Control

- What decision would you want to make for yourself before others had to make it?
- Who would you trust to speak for you if you could not speak?
- What would you want that person to understand about your values?
- Where would you want care to happen if you had a choice?
- What would make medical decisions feel aligned with who you are?
- What treatment would you want people to question if it only prolonged suffering?
- What comfort would matter more to you than control?
- What personal boundary would still matter near the end of life?
- What would you want written down so your family did not have to guess?
- How do you want love and choice to work together in a crisis?

## Spiritual Peace and Meaning

- What helps you feel that your life has meaning?
- What spiritual question becomes louder when you think about death?
- What do you hope is true after death?
- Where do you feel closest to peace?
- What belief has carried you through fear?
- What doubt do you wish you could talk about without being judged?
- What would make your life feel complete enough?
- What part of nature, faith, ritual, or silence comforts you?
- What mystery are you willing to leave unanswered?
- What blessing would you want to give or receive before the end?

## Regret

- What choice would you revisit if you could do it with what you know now?
- What did you spend too much energy proving?
- What did fear keep you from saying, trying, or changing?
- What relationship did you neglect longer than you meant to?
- What would you regret not making time for?
- What version of success might feel empty at the end?
- What did you postpone as if time were guaranteed?
- What would you forgive yourself for if your time were shorter than expected?
- What old dream still asks to be honored in some small way?
- What would you want to stop pretending does not matter?

## Identity Beyond Illness

- What would you want people to remember about you besides what happened to you?
- How can someone stay themselves when illness, grief, or caregiving changes their life?
- What part of your personality would you want protected during a hard season?
- What do people miss when they only see someone as sick?
- What would help you feel normal when life is not normal?
- What name, role, or identity would you not want loss to erase?
- What joy would you still want permission to have during suffering?
- What story about yourself is bigger than your pain?
- How would you want people to talk to you if your body or mind changed?
- What part of you would you hope still came through at the end?

## Family Conflict at the End

- What family disagreement might become sharper under the pressure of illness or loss?
- Who in your family handles hard truth differently from you?
- What would help a family make decisions without turning on each other?
- What old wound might resurface when everyone is scared?
- What does denial protect, and what does it cost?
- How can love still be present when people disagree about care?
- What practical matter could become emotional if it were not discussed early?
- What would you want your family to know about your wishes before they had to choose?
- What pattern would you not want repeated during a crisis?
- What would peace look like if everyone could not agree?
- What would you want your family to remember if dividing your possessions became emotional?

## Rituals and Anniversaries

- What anniversary would you want people to remember gently after you are gone?
- What ritual helps love continue after someone dies?
- What would you want people to do on your birthday after you are gone?
- What place would feel right for remembering someone?
- What song, meal, candle, prayer, or tradition could carry memory?
- How should friends show up on anniversaries of loss?
- What does it mean to you to keep someone's memory alive?
- What family ritual would you want to start before it is needed?
- What would make a memorial feel personal rather than formal?
- What ordinary annual moment might become sacred after loss?

## What Comfort Really Means

- What comforts you when nothing can be fixed?
- What kind of silence feels supportive rather than empty?
- What words would help you feel less alone in fear or grief?
- What words would not help, even if they were well meant?
- What physical comfort, if any, helps you feel safe?
- What familiar thing would you want nearby in a frightening moment?
- What kind of humor still belongs in hard times?
- What does it mean to let someone feel exactly what they feel?
- Who knows how to comfort you without taking over?
- What would comfort look like if it were honest and simple?
- Do you want to be able to contact someone when you can't sleep?

## The Last Conversation

- If you had one final conversation, who would you want it with?
- What would need to be said before goodbye felt complete?
- What would you want someone to stop worrying about?
- What blessing would you want to give?
- What question would you want to ask while there was still time?
- What truth would you want spoken gently and clearly, and who would need to hear it?
- What memory would you want to revisit together?
- In the last conversation what apology, thank you, or reassurance would belong?
- What would you want the final tone to be: peaceful, honest, funny, tender, or something else?
- What could still bring comfort if there were not enough time to say everything?

## Life Clarified by Mortality

- What matters more when you remember that life is temporary?
- What matters less than you used to think?
- What would you change if you believed time was not guaranteed?
- Who would you spend more ordinary time with?
- What courage does mortality ask of you?
- What kind of life would feel honest if it ended sooner than expected?
- What would you stop waiting to enjoy?
- What relationship would you treat with more care?
- What truth about your priorities is hard to ignore?
- What does thinking about death teach you about how to live today?
