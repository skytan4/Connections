Inspired by research on closeness

Deeper Conversations is built around a simple research-backed idea: people often feel closer when they share gradually, listen carefully, and respond with real attention.

In a well-known 1997 study, psychologist Arthur Aron and colleagues found that pairs of strangers who answered a series of increasingly personal questions reported greater feelings of closeness than pairs assigned to small-talk tasks.

Deeper Conversations does not copy the original 36 questions. Instead, it builds on the same underlying pattern: start gently, increase depth over time, and use thoughtful follow-ups to help the conversation continue when something meaningful opens up.

The app also reflects broader research on intimacy and connection: self-disclosure matters, but so does feeling understood, accepted, and responded to.

Read the research:
- Aron et al. (1997), "The Experimental Generation of Interpersonal Closeness"
- Laurenceau, Barrett & Pietromonaco (1998), "Intimacy as an Interpersonal Process"
